## [NASA's Artemis II Mission Successfully Transmits 484 GB of Data from Moon](https://dailygalaxy.com/2026/05/nasa-just-beamed-484-gigabytes-from-moon/) ⭐️ 9.0/10

NASA's Artemis II mission successfully transmitted 484 GB of data from the Moon using the O2O laser communication system, achieving a downlink speed of 260 Mbps. This speed significantly surpasses traditional radio frequency methods. This achievement marks a significant advancement in laser communication technology for space exploration, potentially revolutionizing future lunar and Martian missions. The ability for near real-time analysis and public access to high-quality video could enhance scientific collaboration and public engagement. The O2O system, developed by MIT Lincoln Laboratory, allows for high-bandwidth communication, enabling scientists to analyze high-definition images almost in real-time. Ground stations involved in the project include the Jet Propulsion Laboratory and the Australian National University's Mount Stromlo Observatory.

Dav2d has been introduced as the fastest AV2 decoder, significantly enhancing video compression efficiency and performance for streaming applications. This new decoder aims to be small, portable, and very fast across all platforms. This development is significant as it promises to improve streaming efficiency, potentially benefiting content providers and consumers alike. The introduction of Dav2d may facilitate the adoption of AV2, which is expected to outperform its predecessor, AV1. Dav2d is being developed as a cross-platform decoder with a focus on correctness and performance optimizations for various architectures, including x86, ARM, and RISC-V. The project is still in progress, with plans for further performance enhancements.
